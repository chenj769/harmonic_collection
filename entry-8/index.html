Yutaka Nakamura Sakuga
Yutaka Nakamura is one of the most heard and spoken names in the anime and Sakuga community. He is arguably the most popular Japanese animator, as well as the most influential amongst younger animators today. On a site like Sakugabooru (A index of animation archives dedicated to sharing/discussing sakuga) the top rated clip belongs to the work of Nakamura. He’s animated 12 sequences out of the 25 best-rated ones on the site. His presence is not only on animation sites but can be found in general video-sharing platforms like Youtube. Searching up his name will bring you to MADs (Music Anime Douga, or Video) that showcase the work of Nakamura, with videos up to 7.8 million views.

Why is Nakamura so popular? With just one look at Nakamura’s sequences you could probably tell why. He's renowned for his detailed and fluid action animation to his shift in style incorporating 2D effects animation. His work is bombastic, always breaking barriers, evolving, and influencing other animators. He is considered anime’s most influential action animator. The famous “Yutapon cubes” (Cube shaped debris) and Nakamura impact frames are trademarked around his name and style. Whenever Nakamura shows up, it's always a surprise to see how he is continuously inventing, reiterating, and improving on his style and animation. He is an animator who people look forward to not because of his past sequences, but with anticipation of what he’ll do next.
